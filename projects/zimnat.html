The Challenge

Insurance companies face a critical challenge in identifying which products are most relevant to their existing clients. Mass marketing is often inefficient and intrusive. The goal was to build a recommendation engine for Zimnat Insurance that accurately predicts a client's "missing pieces" to optimize cross-selling based on their current portfolio and profile.

The Technical Solution

The solution is a Hybrid Dual-Engine architecture designed for both robustness and high personalization.

Client Profile & History
↓
Bayesian Statistical Engine
CatBoost Personalizer
↓
Weighted Consensus Layer
↓
Anti-Cheat Product Filter
↓
Final Recommendations (Top K)

I chose CatBoost specifically to handle the high cardinality of categorical features without the need for complex pre-processing. The Bayesian Engine acts as a fallback/safety layer, ensuring that even for new clients with little history, the recommendations remain statistically sound.

The Results & Impact

  • Zero Commercial Error: The built-in anti-cheat system ensures 100% of recommendations are products not yet owned by the client.
  • Market Simulation: Developed a Streamlit tool for managers to simulate "what-if" scenarios for new product launches.
  • Precision Marketing: Enabled the transition from generic campaigns to hyper-personalized agent advice.
